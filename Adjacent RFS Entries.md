## The through-line

`[Inference]` "Cursor for PMs," "Dynamic Software Interfaces," and "Software for Agents" sit on one arc in YC's thinking: **agents are becoming primary actors** in the software lifecycle. That forces a rethink of (a) how we decide/specify what to build, (b) how interfaces adapt per user/agent, and (c) how software is designed to be consumed by agents. The RFS's closing line — "As agents increasingly take the first pass at implementation, the way we define and communicate 'what to build' needs to change" — is the connective tissue. See [[The What-to-Build Bottleneck]].
