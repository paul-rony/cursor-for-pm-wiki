# Indie and Bootstrapped Tools

Index of self-funded / bootstrapped products in the "Cursor for PMs" space — companies that have *not* raised institutional VC. (VC-backed players are in [[VC-Backed Companies]].)

> [!NOTE]
> The category's most prominent product — [[ChatPRD]] — is bootstrapped, not venture-backed. That is a notable fact about the space: the poster child took no VC.

## Confirmed bootstrapped / unfunded

* [[ChatPRD]] -- AI copilot for PMs (PRDs, "on-demand CPO"); built solo by Claire Vo. Six figures revenue, 10,000+ PMs.
* [[Featurebase]] -- feedback/roadmap/changelog platform; "bootstrapped and profitable," Tallinn. Public 0→100K ARR journey.
* [[UserJot]] -- feedback + roadmap + changelog with AI consolidation; "funded by customers, not VCs."

## Strong conceptual match, status unconfirmed

* [[Sprntly]] -- pre-launch "AI agents for product managers" doing the full loop (signals → ranked hypotheses → PRD → Figma → Claude Code/Cursor handoff). **Funding/indie status `[Unverified]` — could turn out VC-backed.**

## Other names (cross-reference)

`[Confirmed]` bootstrapped but weaker loop fit (feedback-collection/roadmap, not synthesis-to-spec):
- **Canny (canny.io)** — feedback management, bootstrapped, ~$3.5M ARR (2024).
- **Aha! (aha.io)** — roadmap/strategy incumbent, **bootstrapped, $100M+ ARR, never raised VC.**

`[Unverified]` status: **Sleekplan** (no funding statement found — do not assert indie).

## How these map to the loop

`[Inference]` The indie tier splits cleanly: **spec-authoring** ([[ChatPRD]]) vs. **feedback aggregation** ([[Featurebase]], [[UserJot]], Canny). The aggregation tools are *inputs* to [[The Full Discovery Loop]], not the synthesis-to-spec engine. Only [[Sprntly]] claims the whole loop — and it is pre-launch.
