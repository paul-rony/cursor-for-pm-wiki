# Cursor for Product Managers — Research Vault

A research wiki mapping the **"Cursor for Product Managers"** space — the category Y Combinator named its #1 Spring 2026 Request for Startups. The thesis: AI made writing code cheap, so the bottleneck moved upstream to *figuring out what to build*. This vault catalogs who is building toward that vision, how they are funded, and whether the tools actually help product managers in practice.

> This is an Obsidian-style vault of interlinked atomic markdown notes. It mirrors the structure of the sibling `agent-memory` vault. Notes use `[[wiki-links]]` to connect. **Start at [[Cursor for Product Managers MOC]]** (`Cursor for Product Managers MOC.md`).

## At a glance

- **The ask:** an AI-native system that ingests customer interviews + product/usage data, synthesizes them, recommends *what to build next* with evidence, and emits agent-executable specs. See [[YC Request for Startups]] and [[The Full Discovery Loop]].
- **Author of record:** the YC RFS entry is bylined **Andrew Miklas**, not Diana Hu (a common misattribution — see [[YC Request for Startups]]).
- **State of the market:** no company verifiably delivers the *full* loop yet. The field splits into three layers — AI interview/ingest, feedback synthesis, and roadmap incumbents adding AI. See [[Market Map and the Open Gap]].
- **Do the tools work?** Useful copilot, unreliable autopilot. Real wins in synthesis speed; real failures in hallucination and generic output. See [[User Testimonials]] and [[Where AI PM Tools Fall Short]].
