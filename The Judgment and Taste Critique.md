# The Judgment and Taste Critique

The most consistent intellectual through-line in thoughtful 2025–2026 commentary: AI compresses *artifact production* but cannot supply *judgment*, and over-reliance risks eroding the PM's own instincts. This is the structural reason the failures in [[Where AI PM Tools Fall Short]] recur.

## The verification paradox

> "You can't verify what you don't understand. Without product management skills and domain expertise, AI becomes a sophisticated probabilistic word generator that produces convincing-looking output you can't verify."
> — Product Focus, "The AI Judgment Paradox"

> "AI multiplies expertise but doesn't manufacture it." … "The higher the stakes of your decision, the more verification you need, and the less efficiency AI will provide."
> — Product Focus, "The AI Judgment Paradox"

## Customer intuition can atrophy

`[Synthesis]` A widely echoed warning: "If you use AI to summarize everything, your own customer intuition degrades. Stay connected to raw feedback and customer conversations." This is the human cost of over-automating [[The Full Discovery Loop]] — the PM stops hearing the signal directly.

## AI can't replicate earned credibility

`[Synthesis]` Jeff Gothelf argues AI can produce professional-looking specs, personas, and summaries, but cannot replicate "the judgment behind the artifact" or the authority of a PM saying "I sat with six customers last month and three of them said the exact same thing." His thesis: as AI democratizes PM *outputs*, storytelling and direct customer engagement become the differentiator.

## Taste is the residual human skill

`[Synthesis]` A 638-practitioner-voices analysis (David Haberlah) concludes "conviction, taste, influence, and outcome-orientation remain irreplaceable," and that human editorial judgment is an *appreciating* asset as automation expands. Related cautions surfaced: Joshua Xu (HeyGen) — "Demo value isn't user value"; Aishwarya Naresh Reganti on "double indeterminacy" (unpredictable users *and* unpredictable LLMs).

## Why it matters for the RFS

`[Inference]` This critique is the counter-pressure to [[The What-to-Build Bottleneck]]. Even if a tool perfectly executes the loop, the scarce input is the judgment to *choose* among evidence-backed options and own the outcome. The most defensible expert consensus: AI **raises** the value of PM judgment rather than replacing it — but "AI slop" and intuition atrophy are real, named risks.
