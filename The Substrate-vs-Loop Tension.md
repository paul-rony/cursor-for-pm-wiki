# The Substrate-vs-Loop Tension

The structural reason no one has closed [[The Full Discovery Loop]] yet: the players who *pitch* the whole loop don't own the signal data to feed it, and the players who *own* the signal data don't pitch the whole loop. This note names that asymmetry and asks which side is better positioned to win. Builds on [[Market Map and the Open Gap]] and pairs with [[The Scaling Debate]].

## The two archetypes

`[Inference]` Catalogued players split into two camps that are almost mirror images:

| | **Substrate owners** | **Loop pitchers** |
|---|---|---|
| **Pitch** | Capture/organize signal (notes, feedback, interviews) | "Upload data → what to build → agent-ready spec" |
| **Have** | Proprietary corpus + distribution + traction | A deck and the literal [[YC Request for Startups\|RFS]] framing |
| **Lack** | Stated ambition to recommend *what to build* | The data substrate and shipping product |
| **Examples** | [[Granola]], [[Dovetail]], [[Enterpret]], [[Listen Labs]] | [[Wien AI]], [[Sprntly]], [[Trova]] |

The loop needs both halves. Today they live in different companies.

## Why climbing beats manufacturing the corpus

`[Inference]` Of the two gaps, the substrate owner's is the easier to close. Adding a "recommend what to build" synthesis step on top of a corpus you already own and that already has users is a product extension. Manufacturing a proprietary, trusted signal corpus *after* pitching the spec engine is a cold-start problem — you must win distribution, earn data-sharing trust, and accumulate volume before your synthesis has anything to chew on. **It is easier to climb the stack than to backfill the foundation.** So if anyone backs into the full loop, the prior favors an ingest/synthesis incumbent climbing up over a pre-launch full-loop startup building down.

## Citations are the bridge from copilot to autopilot

`[Inference]` The vault's sharpest negative finding is that AI synthesis isn't *trusted* (the [[Dovetail]] hallucination case in [[Where AI PM Tools Fall Short]]). The substrate owners are the ones best placed to fix this, because trust comes from grounding outputs in source evidence — and they own the source. [[Granola]] 2.0's **inline citations back to source conversations** are exactly this move: not a feature checkbox but a direct answer to the "unreliable autopilot" critique. Evidence traceability is plausibly the gating capability for the loop, and it is a substrate-owner advantage.

## The third actor: absorption

`[Inference]` This tension does not resolve in a vacuum — [[The Scaling Debate]] adds a third party. Foundation-model platforms (Memory, Projects, Agent Skills) can swallow the *synthesis-and-context* step that the loop pitchers are selling, while they cannot easily swallow a proprietary corpus. That sharpens the conclusion: the durable position is **owning signal data**, not owning the prompt-and-synthesis cleverness. It is another reason the substrate side looks stronger than the loop-pitch side.

## The open question

`[Inference]` So the real contest is not "which full-loop startup wins" — it is:

> **Will the loop be closed by a substrate owner climbing up, or by a spec-engine startup acquiring/partnering for a corpus?**

The vault's evidence tilts toward the former: [[Granola]] is already moving up-stack (folder chat, then enterprise context, $1.5B by Mar 2026), while the purest loop pitches ([[Wien AI]], [[Sprntly]], [[Trova]]) remain pre-launch or unproven. Watch the substrate owners' roadmaps for a "recommend what to build" feature — that is the signal that the gap in [[Market Map and the Open Gap]] is closing.
