# User Testimonials

Attributed, real-world accounts of AI PM tools **improving** workflows — answering the question "is it improving their workflows, and if so how and why?" The honest one-line verdict for the whole space: **useful copilot, unreliable autopilot.** For the other side, see [[Where AI PM Tools Fall Short]].

> [!NOTE]
> Quotes are real and attributed. Synthesis (not a verbatim quote) is marked `[Synthesis]`. Source credibility is graded in [[Evidence and Sourcing Notes]]; vendor-hosted testimonials are flagged inline.

## The strongest concrete win: research/feedback synthesis (days → hours)

> "Extracting insights from our user research interviews using Dovetail's new AI features means we can do that in half the time."
> — Milos D., Product Design Manager (Capterra review of [[Dovetail]])

> "This means I'm not spending time editing video clips, downloading and uploading things, or tabbing through all the interviews separately."
> — Emi F., UX Researcher (Capterra, Dovetail)

> "The AI support has been a game changer! Especially with the auto-tagging feature!"
> — Carlos G., Sr. UX Research Manager (Capterra, Dovetail)

`[Synthesis]` A Dovetail consultant (Maegan P., Capterra) noted that reverting to older tools turned work "that previously took me a few hours with Dovetail" into "many days" — the time savings are felt most acutely when removed.

## Beating the blank page (PRD drafting)

In an independent hands-on test of five tools for writing a PRD, [[ChatPRD]] placed second, credited for sounding human and being well-structured:

> "Feels like a human wrote this PRD." … "The most guided process."
> — Tom Leung, PM coach / host of Fireside Product Management

`[Synthesis]` In the same test, **Claude** won for "the most comprehensive, thoughtful, and strategically sound PRD," while **ChatGPT** was "reliable but generic" and **Grok** "thin, generic, and lacked depth" — a useful balanced, single-author comparison.

## End-to-end workflow compression (enthusiastic, not measured)

`[Synthesis]` Lenny Rachitsky's newsletter describes PM Marily Nika's chained workflow (Perplexity for market research → custom GPTs for PRD → v0 for prototyping → Flow/Sora for video), claiming it compresses "weeks of PM work into about 20 minutes." Aspirational practitioner workflow, not an independent measurement.

`[Synthesis]` PM Resh Mouli ("How PMs Can 2x Their Productivity Using AI") gives a concrete tool map: NotebookLM to synthesize interview transcripts into themes; ChatGPT/Claude/Miro AI for thematic roadmaps; meeting tools (Fireflies/Otter) shifting note-taking from memory-based to system-based; v0/Lovable/Bolt/Replit for "idea → prototype → feedback in days, not weeks." He explicitly keeps "Strategy, Judgment, Prioritization, Accountability firmly human."

## Survey-level signal (broad, neutral source)

`[Synthesis]` Lenny Rachitsky & Noam Segal's survey of ~1,750 PMs/engineers/designers/founders reported **55% say AI exceeded expectations, ~70% say it improved work quality, and more than half save at least half a day per week** on their most important tasks. (Headline stats public; detailed breakdown paywalled — treat as directional.)

## What consistently works (themes)

`[Inference]`
- **Synthesis time-compression on already-rich data** — the single most repeated concrete win.
- **Beating the blank page** — drafting PRDs, outlines, roadmaps; framed as scaffolding, not finished artifacts.
- **Mechanical drudgery removal** — meeting notes, transcription, auto-tagging, clip editing.
- **Prototyping speed** — v0/Lovable/Bolt/Replit turning specs into clickable prototypes in minutes/days.
